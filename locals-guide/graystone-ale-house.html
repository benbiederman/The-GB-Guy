Graystone Ale House

Graystone Ale House is a bar/restaurant located in beautiful De Pere, Wisconsin. Established in 2010, Graystone has been serving the De Pere area a wide-variety of food options for nearly a decade. I decided to see what Graystone Ale House was all about.

What to expect

We ended up getting there relatively early on a Friday, despite getting there early, it was already quite busy. We immediately got seated at our table and were greeted by a very friendly waitress. She was definitely the highlight of the venture. Good hospitality isn’t always the easiest to find and she knocked it out.

One thing I found out about Graystone Ale House is that they’re owned by the same people associated with The Bar and Haegemeister Park; which I’m undecided how I feel about that. I had a very bad experience at Haegemeister Park, however, I always thought The Bar was decent. Graystone Ale House, to me, was a very forgettable place.

For my dinner, I ordered a cheeseburger and tater tots (because I’m a 7 year old). The first thing I noticed was the small quantity of tater tots you get. Someone online even called out the minimum amount of tots, I received. Napoleon Dynamite would not be hyped about it. I bet you didn’t expect a reference from a 2004 movie in a food review did you?! The burger was decent, but as I said before, the entire meal was quite forgettable. In fact, if not for my notes I jotted down, I’d struggle with this review since I’m doing it a few days after eating there.

One thing that may appeal to individuals coming to Green Bay during a Packers game is that Graystone offers a shuttle to Lambeau Field. That’s a nice little perk. I’m not sure how many places in the area offer that, but it’s always good to have the options.

Overall, I’d eat at Graystone again, it wasn’t bad. However, I don’t see myself going there again anytime soon because there are so many other options in the area I’d rather go to instead.

Rating

3.75
